CPU

CPU (Central Processing Unit): The CPU is the "brain" of the computer that performs all the calculations and manages all the other components. It determines how fast your computer can process data and run applications, making it a critical component for gaming performance.

Motherboard

Motherboard: The motherboard is the main circuit board that connects all the other components together, such as the CPU, memory, and video card. It determines what kind of CPU and memory you can use and how many components you can install. It also contains features such as USB and audio ports and provides power to all the other components.

Graphics Card

Video Card (Graphics Card): The video card is responsible for rendering images on your screen and is an essential component for gaming. It determines the quality and speed of your game's graphics, making it critical for a smooth and visually impressive gaming experience.

Memory

Memory (RAM): Memory, also known as RAM (Random Access Memory), is where your computer stores data that it needs to access quickly. It allows your computer to run multiple applications simultaneously and ensures smooth gaming performance by providing quick access to game data.

Power Supply

Power Supply (PSU): The power supply is responsible for supplying power to all the other components in your computer. It determines the overall power capacity of your computer and must be able to handle the power requirements of your other components, including the CPU, video card, and storage.

Storage

Storage: Storage is where your computer stores data permanently, including your operating system, applications, and games. There are two main types of storage: hard disk drives (HDDs) and solid-state drives (SSDs). SSDs are faster and more expensive, while HDDs are slower and cheaper but offer more storage capacity.

Case

Case: The case is the enclosure that houses all the other components and protects them from damage. It also determines the size and style of your computer and may include features such as cooling fans and LED lighting.

Operating System

Operating System (OS): The operating system is the software that manages all the other components and provides a user interface for interacting with your computer. Windows is the most popular operating system for gaming, but macOS and Linux are also viable options depending on your preferences and needs.
